How To Use
Tap a day tab at the top to jump to today's session. Each strength workout expands to show the full lift list — tap the set boxes to mark them off as you go.
Alternating sessions: Monday rotates between an upper-body lift and a boxing session, and Saturday rotates between Kettlebell A (conditioning) and B (strength). Track which one you did last week and switch.
Set check-offs reset when you close the tab. They're for tracking the current session, not long-term history.

Tuesday · Conditioning
Boxing
Skill work, combinations, rounds on bag or pads
60–75 min High intensity
Structure: Warm-up + skipping → shadow boxing → technique drills → bag/pad rounds → conditioning finisher. Treat this as your hardest CV session of the week.
Recovery flag: If you boxed Monday too, expect Tuesday to feel rough. Hydrate hard and prioritise carbs around this session.
Wednesday · Mixed
Strength & Conditioning
Mixed-modal session + light walk to finish
40–50 min Moderate
Sample circuit (5 rounds, 45s on / 15s off): goblet squat → push-up → KB swing → row → mountain climbers. Followed by a 15–20 min walk.
Why the walk: active recovery between two harder days (Tue boxing + Thu lower). Don't skip it — it's working.

Sunday · Aerobic
Long Easy Run
Build duration progressively across the 12 weeks
Wks 1–4 · 30–45 min Wks 5–8 · 45–60 Wks 9–12 · 60–75
Easy means easy. Conversational pace — Zone 2. If you're gasping, slow down. The point is to build aerobic base without taxing your ability to box and lift.
Fasted or fed? Either works for fat loss at this intensity. If you go fasted, keep it under 60 min and prioritise protein within an hour after.
